Q2:

$$
\begin{aligned}y’&=x\ln \left( x\right) ,y\left( 1\right) =2\
y’&=x\ln \left( x\right) \
\int dy&=\color{blue}{\int x\ln \left( x\right) dx}\end{aligned}
$$

Solving for RHS (using DI method):

$D$ $I$
$+$ $\ln(x)$ $x$
$-$ $\frac{1}{x}$ $\frac{x^2}{2}$

$$
\begin{aligned}&\color{blue}{ \int x\ln \left( x\right) dx }\
&=\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\ln \left( x\right) -\int \dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\cdot \dfrac{1}{x}dx\
&=\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\ln \left( x\right) -\dfrac{1}{4}x^{2}+C\end{aligned}
$$

Then we have:
$$
\begin{aligned}
y&=\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\ln \left( x\right) -\dfrac{1}{4}x^{2}+c\
y&=\dfrac{1^{2}}{2}\ln \left( 1\right) -\dfrac{1}{4}\cdot 1^{2}+c=2\
2&=-\dfrac{1}{4}+c\
c&=\dfrac{9}{4}
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus: $y\left( x\right) =\dfrac{x^{2}}{2}\ln \left( x\right) -\dfrac{1}{4}x^{2}+\dfrac{9}{4}$

Q3:

Given: $\dfrac{dh}{dt}=-K\sqrt{h}$ , when $t=0$ ,$z=h_{0}$
$$
\begin{aligned}\dfrac{dh}{dt}&=-K\sqrt{h}\
\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{h}}dh&=\int -K dt\
\int h^{-\frac{1}{2}}dh&=-Kt+C\
\dfrac{h^{-\frac{1}{2}+1}}{-\frac{1}{2}+1}&=-Kt+C\
2h^{\frac{1}{2}}&=-Kt+C\
h&=\left( \frac{-Kt+C}{2}\right) ^2
\end{aligned}
$$
when $t=0$ :
$$
\begin{aligned}h=\left( \dfrac{C}{2}\right) ^{2}&=h_{0}&\
C^{2}&=4h_{0}&\text{(Since } h_{0} \text{ is positive)}\
C&=2\sqrt{h_{0}}&\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, $h=\left( \dfrac{-Kt+2\sqrt{h_{0}}}{2}\right) ^{2}$

Next find time taken $t$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
h=\left( \dfrac{-Kt+2\sqrt{h_{0}}}{2}\right) ^{2}&=0\
-Kt+2\sqrt{h_{0}}&=0\
Kt&=2\sqrt{h_{0}}\
t&=\dfrac{2\sqrt{h_{0}}}{K}\end{aligned}
$$

Q4:

First: $y’’+2y’=2e^{-x}+4$

​ let $z\left(x\right)=y’$

​ $z^{2}+2z=2e^{-x}+4$

​ solve :
$$
\begin{aligned}z^{2}+2z&=0\
z\left( z+2\right) &=0\
z_{1}&=0\
z_{2}&=-2
\end{aligned}
$$
​ Thus:
$$
\begin{aligned}Y_{c}\left( x\right) &=C_{1}e^{0x}+C_{2}e^{-2x}=C_{1}+C_{2}e^{-2x}\
Y_{p}\left( x\right) &=Ae^{-x}+Bx\
Y’{p}\left( x\right) &=-Ae^{-x}\
Y’’{p}\left( x\right) &=Ae^{-x}
\end{aligned}
$$
​ Then:
$$
\begin{aligned}Y’’{p}\left( x\right) +2Y’{p}\left( x\right) &=2e^{-x}+4\
Ae^{-x}+2\left( -Ae^{-x}+B\right) &=2e^{-x}+4\
Ae^{-x}-2Ae^{-x}+2B&=2e^{-x}+4\
Ae^{-x}-2Ae^{-x}&=2e^{-x}\
-Ae^{-x}&=2e^{-x}\
A&=-2\
2B&=4\
B&=2
\end{aligned}
$$
​ Therefore: $Y_{p}\left(x\right)=-2e^{-x}+2x$ , and $Y\left( x\right) =Y_{c}\left( x\right) +Y_{p}\left( x\right) =C_{1}+C_{2}e^{-2x}-2e^{-x}+2x$

When $y\left( 0\right) =y’\left( 0\right) =0$ ,equation becomes:
$$
\begin{aligned}
Y=0&=C_{1}+C_{2}e^{-2\cdot 0}-2e^{-0}+2\cdot 0\
0&=C_{1}+C_{2}-2\
Y’&=C_{2}\left( -2\right) e^{-2x}-2\left( -1\right) e^{-x}+2\
0&=-2C_{2}e^{-2\cdot 0}+2e^{-0}+2\
0&=-2C_{2}+4\
C_{2}&=2\
C_{1}&=0
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence, $Y=2e^{-2x}-2e^{-x}+2x$

Q5:

First, $n\neq 0,1$
$$
\begin{aligned}y’+p\left( x\right) y&=q\left( x\right) y^{n}\
\dfrac{y’}{y^{n}}+\dfrac{p\left( x\right) }{y^{n-1}}&=q\left( x\right) \
y^{-n}y^{-1}+p\left( x\right) y^{1-n}&=q\left( x\right) \end{aligned}
$$
​ let $u\left(x\right) = y^{1-n}$
$$
\begin{aligned}u’\left( x\right) &=\left( 1-n\right) y^{-n}y’\
\dfrac{n’}{1-n}&=y^{-n}y’\
\dfrac{u’}{1-n}+p\left( x\right) u&=q\left( x\right)& \text{Since }u\left(x\right) = y^{1-n} \text{ and }\dfrac{n’}{1-n}&=y^{-n}y’\
u’+\left( 1-n\right) p\left( x\right) u&=q\left( x\right) \left( 1-n\right)
\end{aligned}
$$
Next we find $y’+2y=7xy^{2}$

let $u=y^{1-n}$ and $n=2$ ,then we have
$$
\begin{aligned}u&=y^{1-2}=y^{-1}=\dfrac{1}{y}\
u’&=-y^{-2}y’\
-u’&=\dfrac{y’}{y^{2}}\end{aligned}
$$
Then $y’+2y=7xy^{2}$ becomes
$$
\begin{aligned}\dfrac{y’}{y^{2}}+\dfrac{2y}{y^{2}}&=7x\
\dfrac{y’}{y^{2}}+\dfrac{2}{y}&=7x\
-u’+2u&=7x\
u’-2u&=-7x\end{aligned}
$$
Let $v=e^{\int -2dx}$ , $v=e^{-2x}$
$$
\begin{aligned}
u’-2u&=-7x\
e^{-2x}u’-2e^{-2x}u&=-7x\left( e^{-2x}\right) \
e^{-2x}u&=\color{blue}{\int -7x\left( e^{-2x}\right) dx}\end{aligned}
$$
Solving RHS: $\color{blue}{\int -7x\left( e^{-2x}\right) dx=-7\int x\left( e^{-2x}\right) dx}$

$D$ $I$
$+$ $x$ $e^{-2x}$
$-$ $1$ $\frac{e^{-2x}}{-2}$
$+$ $0$ $\frac{e^{-2x}}{4}$

$\color{blue}{\int 7x\left( e^{-2x}\right) dx=-7\left( -\dfrac{1}{2}e^{-2x}x-\dfrac{e^{-2x}}{4}+C\right) }$

Since $u=\frac{1}{y}$
$$
\begin{aligned}e^{-2x}\cdot \dfrac{1}{y}&=-7\left( -\dfrac{1}{2}e^{-2x}x-\dfrac{1}{4}e^{-2x}+C\right) \
y&=\frac{e^{-2x}}{\frac{7}{2}e^{-2x}+\frac{7}{4}e^{-2x}-7C}\
y& =\frac{4 e^{-2 x}}{7\left(3 e^{-2 x}-4 c\right)}
\end{aligned}
